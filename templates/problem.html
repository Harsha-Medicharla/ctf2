Sherlock’s Journey

On a stormy night in London, Sherlock Holmes decided to take a long and grueling journey to solve a mysterious case that had been troubling Scotland Yard. Each day, he would walk a certain number of miles in a specific pattern to clear his mind and gather clues.

On the first day, Sherlock would walk a miles, on the second day, he would walk b miles, and on the third day, he would walk c miles. From there on, his walking pattern would repeat, with Sherlock walking a miles on the fourth day, b miles on the fifth, c miles on the sixth, and so on.

Sherlock will complete his journey when he has walked at least n miles in total. Your task is to determine the exact day on which Sherlock will complete his journey.

Problem Description

You are given four integers n, a, b, and c:

  • n is the total distance Sherlock wants to cover in kilometers.
  • a, b, and c represent the number of miles Sherlock walks on the first, second, and third days respectively.

Your task is to determine the exact day Sherlock will have walked at least n kilometers in total, completing his journey.

Input

The first line contains one integer t (1 ≤ t ≤ 1000) — the number of test cases.

For each test case:

  • The first line contains an integer n (1 ≤ n ≤ 10⁹) — the total distance Sherlock wants to walk.
  • The second line contains three integers a, b, and c (1 ≤ a, b, c ≤ 10⁶) — the distances Sherlock walks on the first, second, and third days, respectively.

Output

For each test case, output one integer — the day on which Sherlock will have walked at least n kilometers in total, completing his journey.

Test Case 

    Input:
    4
    12 1 5 3
    6 6 7 4
    16 3 4 1

    Output:
    5
    1
    6

Explanation

  • Test Case 1: Over the first four days, Sherlock walks 1+5+3+1 = 10 kilometers. On the fifth day, he walks another 5 kilometers, making his total distance 15 kilometers. Since 12 kilometers are needed, Sherlock will complete his journey on the fifth day.
  • Test Case 2: In the second test case, Sherlock will walk 6 kilometers on the first day, which is exactly n=6, completing the journey on the first day.
  • Test Case 3: Sherlock will walk 3+4+1=8 kilometers in the first cycle. Then the next 8 kilometers after repeating the cycle completes on the 6th day, with the total 16 kilometers.
