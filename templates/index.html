Sherlock Holmes: The Mystery of the Midnight Cipher

It was a stormy night, and the streets of London were shrouded in a thick fog. At 221B Baker Street, Sherlock Holmes and Dr. Watson sat by the fire, examining a peculiar letter that had arrived earlier that evening.

“This, Watson, is no ordinary correspondence,” said Holmes, holding the parchment up to the light. “Its sender has gone to great lengths to obscure its meaning. Notice the smudges here—they suggest the use of invisible ink, activated by heat.”

Watson leaned forward, intrigued. “And the message itself? What does it say?”

Holmes smiled faintly. “It’s a cipher, my dear friend. The words appear random, but they hide a deeper truth. Listen to this:

'In the chimes of midnight lies the key,
Seek the numbers, one, two, and three.
Combine their sum with the letter’s plea.'
